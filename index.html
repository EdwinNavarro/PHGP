We are a nonprofit organization aimed at spreading awareness and combating risk of diabetes

Our mission is to improve San Jose residents overall public health by creating a welcoming and inclusive environment for all individuals. We believe that by continuing to spread awareness, more people will begin to pay attention to their lifestyles and start to analyze how their eating habits and physical activity are positively or negatively impacting their bodies. As a team, we want to ensure that all people, regardless of age, gender, race, and personal attributes, are included in all of our provided services.

At DiabetesCombatants we have all the information to help you prevent diabetes

Preventative Actions

There are various ways to help prevent diabetes. It is important to maintain a balance in an individual's diet. The reason it is vital to eat a small portion of all kinds of foods is because it is easier to maintain a healthier lifestyle. When a person restricts themselves from eating all foods that contain high amounts of sugar, it is far more likely that they will develop eating disorders and among those disorders is the most common disorder which is binge eating. It is very probable to become obese once a person gets tired of being restricted from foods that they enjoy. Once a person becomes tired of it, they will eat large amounts of fatty foods to ease their anxiety. Not only will it make them more prone to obesity, but it will also make them more prone to diabetes.

Our Services

Services

Diabetes is a chronic disease and stands among some of the biggest challenges for America. DiabetesCombatants provides
education and training at all levels of diabetes. We will assist participants to learn how to take the best care of themselves

Services to Enjoy

Diabetes Combatants will provide some courses about type 1 and type 2 diabetes and some related courses about the disease. Our services consist of a healthcare team to help people to stay healthy and provide the practical application of learning from the program. Nutrition counseling, development of healthy recipes, meal planning, the intervention of physical. Our registered dietitian nutritionist provides a nutrition care plan for a diabetic person and provides counseling regarding their healthy eating plan and food choices. The healthcare teams teach skills of self-management such as healthy eating, monitoring of glucose level, and medication adherence, tackle problems, maintenance of active lifestyle, reduce onset complications of diabetes and deal with emotional trauma. to diabetes.

Integrating Personal trainers

The introduction of our physical fitness training program will help participants keep a record of their weight and prevent further complications. The weight can be maintained with the intervention of lifestyle changes like physical activity. Guest speakers and counselors are also invited for online talks or physical conferences to help people develop healthy lifestyles. Listening to motivational speakers and diabetes experts and their experience adds knowledge and positivity to deal with the disease.

The organizational structure includes an advisory group, population served, quality coordinator, and quality staff teams. An online application is submitted for participation. Diabetic patients can talk with our staff members from Monday through Friday between business hours. The service provides answers to all received emails and calls. The disease is encountered by all the people either in themselves or in their loved ones.

Almost 2000 people seek the services of DiabetesCombatants each month to educate themselves about the topic of diabetes, its health effects, treatments, and the latest research in the area. Not necessarily people with diabetes reach Diabetes Combatants, but also other people who have diabetics in their families or those currently working on the disease. Most of the participants receiving services of Diabetes Combatants are people of the age group about from 45 to 65 which are at the highest risk and onset of diabetes type 2 (Mehrabian et al., 2012.

Younger adults of ages about 20 to 40 suffering from diabetes type 1 (Rogers, Kim, Banerjee, & Lee, 2017) are also the other participating group of Diabetes Combatants. Diabetes Combatants provides resources and education to the people living with diabetes type 1 or diabetes type 2. Diabetes Combatants conveys the message, stories, and experiences of the people living with diabetes to the national health policy-making forums and other communities to help create national conservations.

Apart from providing the latest research information and educating people about diabetes, DiabetesCombatants also helps health advisors and counselors from all around the world to reach diabetic people through its website to guide and help them regarding the disease. The healthcare providers available for the people with diabetes through Diabetes Combatants can have different appointment times according to the needs of the patients. They help people not only in the diagnosis of the disease but also in designing a lifestyle that will prolong their lifespan and improve their health.

Apart from diabetes, people with obesity can also reach Diabetes Combatants to seek guidance to control their abnormal body weights and have a healthy lifestyle. DiabetesCombatants can be joined via Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn. Additional email is also provided on the website of Diabetes Combatants for more information.
